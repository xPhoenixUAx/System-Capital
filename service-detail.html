Delivery process

How we move from project context to a launched digital system.

Our process is built for companies that need clarity before execution: structured intake, defined scope, purposeful design, clean development, launch checks and practical support after release.

PrinciplesTimelineHandoffsCommunicationFAQ

Why the process matters

Good digital work fails when decisions are disconnected.

A website or digital growth project is rarely only a design task. It involves business goals, service positioning, content, user experience, development, form handling, analytics, launch timing and post-launch updates.

Our process keeps those layers connected. We do not move straight into visuals before the page purpose is clear, and we do not launch a build before the forms, mobile behavior and essential checks are reviewed.

The result is calmer delivery for the client: fewer blind spots, clearer approvals and a website system that is easier to maintain after launch.

Project timeline

Five stages from enquiry to improvement.

The exact timeline depends on project size, content readiness and technical complexity. The sequence stays consistent so decisions have a clear place.

01

Design direction

We translate the strategy into responsive layouts, visual rhythm, typography, image direction, CTA placement and reusable sections.

  • Hero and key pages
  • Component direction

04

Development and QA

The approved design becomes a working website with frontend implementation, practical backend form handling and launch checks.

  • Responsive build
  • PHP form processing where needed
  • Browser, link and form testing
05

Launch and support

We prepare the release, review priority pages and define what should be monitored or improved after the website is live.

  • Launch checklist
  • Post-launch fixes
  • Improvement backlog

FAQ

Process questions before starting

Short answers about how work begins, how scope is defined and how communication is handled.

How do we start a project?

Submit the project form with goals, service interest, company details, budget range and project notes. We review the context and respond by email.

Can the process change for small projects?

Yes. A landing page does not need the same depth as a larger website system. The sequence stays structured, but the number of review points and deliverables can be lighter.

When does design begin?

Design begins after the page purpose, content direction, service structure and basic scope are clear enough to avoid guessing.

What if we do not have all content ready?

We can help structure the content and identify missing items. If major content is missing, the timeline may be adjusted to avoid building around placeholders.

What happens after launch?

We can provide post-launch support, technical checks, content updates, conversion refinements and campaign page adjustments depending on the agreed scope.
