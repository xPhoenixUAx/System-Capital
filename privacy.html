Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data System Capital Management, s.r.o. collects through this website, how it is used, and what rights you have under applicable data protection laws.

Last updated: 1 January 2026

We provide website creation, web design, frontend and backend development, digital marketing systems, launch support, and related business support services to companies across Europe and internationally.

For the purposes of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and applicable Slovak data protection legislation, System Capital Management, s.r.o. acts as the data controller in respect of personal data collected through this website.

We are committed to protecting the personal data of our website visitors, prospective clients, and business partners. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

02. Data We Collect

We may collect personal data when you interact with our website, submit a form, communicate with us, or engage our services.

The types of data we may collect include:

  • Identity data - name, surname, company name, and job title where provided.
  • Contact data - email address, company address, and business communication details.
  • Financial data - invoicing details, VAT numbers, and billing-related information where applicable.
  • Technical data - IP address, browser type, device information, time zone, and system data.
  • Usage data - pages visited, time on site, navigation paths, and website interactions.
  • Marketing data - preferences for receiving communications and campaign-related interactions.
  • Business data - information shared in relation to services, including project goals, budgets, timelines, technical requirements, and project scope.

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data. If such data is provided, it will be deleted where appropriate.

03. How We Use Your Data

We may use personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to enquiries and contact requests submitted through the website.
  • To evaluate project fit, prepare proposals, and provide our services.
  • To manage client relationships and business communications.
  • To process invoices, payments, and business transactions.
  • To analyse website performance and user behaviour.
  • To support marketing, advertising, and website improvement activities.
  • To ensure website security, prevent misuse, and maintain service reliability.
  • To comply with legal, accounting, tax, and regulatory obligations.

We do not use your data for unrelated purposes without informing you where required by law.

04. Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases under the GDPR:

  • Contract performance - when processing is necessary to provide requested services or take steps before entering into a contract.
  • Legitimate interests - to operate our business, respond to enquiries, improve services, ensure website security, and manage client relationships.
  • Legal obligation - to comply with applicable laws, accounting obligations, tax rules, and regulatory requirements.
  • Consent - where required, including for certain marketing communications or non-essential cookies.

You may withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.

05. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, including legal, accounting, and business requirements.

  • Client and financial data - generally up to 7 years where required for legal, tax, or accounting purposes.
  • Enquiry data - generally up to 2 years, unless a longer period is necessary for ongoing communication or business records.
  • Analytics data - generally up to 26 months, depending on the analytics tool configuration.
  • Marketing data - until consent is withdrawn or the data is no longer required.

We may anonymise data for analytical and reporting purposes.

06. Sharing Your Data

We do not sell your personal data.

We may share data with trusted third parties where necessary, including:

  • Service providers such as hosting, email, analytics, CRM, and website infrastructure providers.
  • Advertising and analytics platforms, such as Google or Meta, where applicable.
  • Professional advisers, including legal, accounting, and tax advisers.
  • Authorities, regulators, or courts where required by law.

All third parties are required to handle personal data securely and lawfully.

07. International Transfers

Some service providers may process personal data outside the European Economic Area.

Where this occurs, we use appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), approved data transfer mechanisms, or other safeguards required by applicable data protection laws.

08. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your data.
  • Restrict processing of your data.
  • Receive your data in a portable format.
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details below.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Slovak supervisory authority: the Office for Personal Data Protection of the Slovak Republic.
